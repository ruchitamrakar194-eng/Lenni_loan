SCOPE OF AGREEMENT 2.1 The Service Provider is a registered credit provider that provides salary-linked and short-term credit facilities in compliance with the provisions of the National Credit Act and all applicable South African legislation. 2.2 The Employer agrees, subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement and subject to the written consent and/or authority of the relevant employee, to assist in facilitating and administering authorised salary deductions in respect of loan instalments due by eligible employees to Lenni (Pty) Ltd. 2.3 The Parties acknowledge that all salary deductions shall be authorised by the employee in accordance with applicable legislation, including: the Basic Conditions of Employment Act;  the National Credit Act; and  Information Regulator POPIA requirements.  3. RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE SERVICE PROVIDER The Service Provider hereby undertakes and warrants that: 3.1 It shall maintain all registrations, licences, and approvals required to operate as a credit provider in South Africa. 3.2 It shall conduct affordability assessments and credit vetting in accordance with the National Credit Act. 3.3 It shall ensure that all employee loan applications and salary deductions are properly authorised by the employee. 3.4 It shall maintain appropriate systems and controls to protect confidential and personal information processed under this Agreement. 3.5 It shall administer and reconcile all payments received from the Employer in respect of employee loan repayments. 3.6 It shall provide statements or certificates of balance upon reasonable request. 3.7 The Employer shall not be liable for repayment of any employee debt other than its obligation to deduct authorised instalments and remit same to the Service Provider. 4. RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE EMPLOYER The Employer hereby undertakes that: 4.1 It shall deduct authorised loan instalments from the salaries of participating employees where valid written authority has been provided. 4.2 It shall remit deducted amounts to the Service Provider within the agreed payment period. 4.3 It shall notify the Service Provider as soon as reasonably possible where: an employee resigns;  employment is terminated;  salary payments are suspended; or  payroll restructuring may affect deductions.  4.4 It shall exercise reasonable care in administering payroll deductions. 4.5 The Employer shall not disclose confidential information relating to the Service Provider or its clients except where legally required. 4.6 The Employer acknowledges that participation in the salary deduction arrangement does not make the Employer liable for the employee’s debt obligations. 5. POPIA AND CONFIDENTIALITY 5.1 The Parties acknowledge that they may process personal information in terms of this Agreement and undertake to comply with the provisions of the Information Regulator Protection of Personal Information Act (“POPIA”). 5.2 The Employer may provide limited employee verification information, including employee numbers and employment confirmation details, strictly for purposes related to: employment verification;  payroll deduction administration;  affordability assessments; and  fraud prevention.  5.3 The Service Provider undertakes to process all personal information lawfully, securely, and only for authorised purposes. 5.4 Employees shall provide consent during the loan application process for the verification and processing of relevant employment information. 6. COMMENCEMENT, DURATION AND TERMINATION 6.1 This Agreement shall commence on the date of signature by the last signing Party. 6.2 This Agreement shall continue indefinitely unless terminated by either Party upon 60 (sixty) days written notice. 6.3 Termination shall not affect: existing authorised deductions;  outstanding employee obligations; or  accrued rights of either Party.  7. LIABILITY 7.1 Neither Party shall be liable for indirect or consequential damages, including loss of profits or business interruption. 7.2 The Service Provider indemnifies the Employer against losses arising from: unlawful credit granting;  regulatory non-compliance by the Service Provider; or  unauthorised deductions caused by the Service Provider.  7.3 The Employer indemnifies the Service Provider against losses arising from: fraud;  wilful misconduct; or  negligent payroll administration by the Employer or its staff.  8.
